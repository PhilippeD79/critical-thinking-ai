## The Sequences

Three alerts to discover, one mindset to build. Each sequence exists in versions adapted for middle school and high school students (ages 12-18).

### Sequence 01: Detecting AI Writing Style

AI has recognizable writing tics. Students discover them by analyzing texts they've generated themselves.

**Key learning**: Students learn to identify typical patterns in AI-generated text and understand that AI has a distinctive "signature."

---

### Sequence 02: Can AI Lie? Setting Traps

AI can confidently assert false information. Students set traps to prove it.

**Key learning**: Students discover that AI can "hallucinate" facts and that confidence in delivery doesn't equal accuracy.

---

### Sequence 03: Becoming an AI Pilot

AI doesn't reasonâ€”it predicts. Students observe this and create their own usage charter.

**Key learning**: Understanding the fundamental difference between statistical prediction and logical reasoning. Students develop their own critical guidelines.

---

### Sequence 04: Detecting Bias

AI can reproduce and amplify stereotypes. Students learn to detect them.

**Key learning**: Awareness that AI models reflect and can magnify existing societal biases. Critical evaluation of AI outputs through a lens of fairness and representation.

### Case Study: When Claude Invents My Name

The inspector teaching critical thinking about AI discovers that Claude successively calls him "Philippe Music" then "Philippe Dagobert"... A perfect case of hallucination analyzed using the A.U.D.I.T. protocol tools.

**What makes this powerful**: This is an authentic example that happened during the development of this very framework. The creator's own experience with AI hallucination becomes a teaching tool.

### Alert: AI Companions - An Invisible Phenomenon

Millions of adolescents are developing emotional connections with conversational AI (Replika, Character.AI, MyIA...). A massive but little-known phenomenon that directly concerns our students. This resource helps teachers understand the mechanisms, identify risks, and implement preventive actions.

### The "Columbo Teacher"

Rethinking mathematics education in the age of artificial intelligence. From the "Sherlock Holmes" model (the student-investigator searching for solutions) to the "Columbo" model (the student-validator analyzing answers). A reflection informed by 18 theoretical frameworks from educational research.

**Core insight**: In the AI era, the pedagogical challenge shifts from raw production to critical analysis and validation of machine-generated solutions.

## The A.U.D.I.T. Acronym

**A**nalyze - Examine AI outputs critically  
**U**se your knowledge - Apply disciplinary expertise  
**D**oubt - Maintain healthy skepticism  
**I**nterrogate limits - Test AI boundaries  
**T**est - Verify and validate

This five-step framework provides students with a systematic approach to interacting with AI tools across all disciplines.
